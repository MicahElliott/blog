About

Thanks for visiting my blog! My name is Micah Elliott, and I’m an entrepreneur and founder of Membean: an online vocabulary training program used by schools in all 50 states and around the globe. I presently manage our infrastructure, train teachers, create podcasts and other content, and even do some development.

My goal for this blog is to share knowledge and discoveries

Beyond Membean, my interests include programming in Clojure, learning about statistics (mostly with R), triathlons and marathons, and bookkeeping at my kids’ baseball games.

Colophon

This blog comprises a mix of my favorite tech (which I also like to write about). It’s hosted on FreeBSD 10.1 by a DigitalOcean droplet running Nginx, and is deployed via Ansible. The blog engine is Cryogen, written in Clojure, and it is nice to look at because it came that way (and thanks to Bootstrap). Posts are written in Asciidoc with Vim. Changes are managed and shared on Github. Read my recipe on how to set it up if you want one, too.

Videos here are captured with ssr (simple screen recorder) or tty2gif. They are sometimes edited with KDenLive. Audio is recorded and edited with Audacity.
